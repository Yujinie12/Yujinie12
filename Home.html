All of Us Are Dead

If you haven't tuned into All Of Us Are Dead , what are you waiting for? Seriously. In only two weeks, the K-drama series has become the next big thing on social media. With tons of drama, an apocalyptic plague, and even a little love story hidden throughout the 12-episodes, All Of Us Are Dead has all the makings of a perfectly binge-able, must-watch series.


Don't believe me? If high school isn't hard enough, a group of students are forced to go into Hyosan High School after a virus outbreak emerges in Hyosan City, turning most infected people into mindless, flesh-eating zombies. Others, who retain their normal human looks and mobility, never fully transform into zombies. Nicknamed Halfbies, these human and zombie hybrids function normally, but they gain enhanced abilities like super-strength and hearing. With tons of twists and turns, the show kept viewers on its toes, sharing their thoughts on social media. If you're wondering how season one of All Of Us Are Dead ends, read ahead for everything we know so far.

Note:

All Of Us Are Dead is based on the popular Naver webtoon of the same name by webtoon creator Joo Dong-geun. Webtoons are vertical, continuous strips of mobile-friendly illustrated stories that can span genres from romance to horror.


Development

On April 12, 2020, Netflix announced through a press release that JTBC Studios and Film Monster would produce a series called All of Us Are Dead based on the popular webtoon Now at Our School


Casting

On April 19, 2020, Yoon Chan-young was confirmed to star in the series as one of the students.Park Ji-hu joined the main cast on April 22. On July 1, they were officially joined by Cho Yi-hyun, Lomon and Yoo In-soo.In selecting the cast, director Lee Jae-kyoo said, "I thought that bringing on actors who were great at acting but still unknown to the public would add to the immersion of the series"
